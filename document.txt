Week 2 
Module Description
	•	Goal: Help business grow.
	•	Key points:
	1.	You don’t have to sell to everyone.
	2.	Use the STP model – Segmentation, Targeting, and Positioning.
	•	Identify most valuable customer groups.
	•	Sell products that meet their exact needs.

Why STP?
	•	Not everyone is your customer.
	•	You can’t be all things to all people.
	•	Be specific about who your customers are and serve their needs.

This Module Helps In:
	1.	Understanding different approaches to market segmentation.
	2.	Targeting and positioning.
	3.	Assessing segment attractiveness.
	4.	Creating customer value through differentiation.

Key Questions in Marketing Strategy
	1.	Which customers will we serve?
	2.	How will we serve them?

Process
	•	Segmentation: Divide the total market into smaller segments.
	•	Targeting: Select the segment(s) to enter.
	•	Differentiation: Create superior customer value.
	•	Positioning: Position the offering in the minds of target customers.

Market Segmentation
	•	Purpose:
	1.	Meet each segment’s unique needs.
	2.	Optimize resources and improve customer satisfaction.
	•	Example: Singapore Airlines
	•	Singapore Airlines → Premium long-haul
	•	SilkAir → Medium service
	•	Scoot → Low-budget long haul
	•	Tiger → Budget medium

Bases of Segmentation
	1.	Why — Needs, decisions, preferences (convert non-loyal into loyal).
	2.	What — Usage, loyalty, profitability (Recency, Frequency, Monetary).
	3.	Who — Demographics, lifestyle, media habits, etc.

Market Segmentation Bases
	1.	Geographic – Country, region, city.
	2.	Demographic – Age, income, gender, family size, occupation, education.
	3.	Psychographic – Lifestyle, personality.
	4.	Behavioral – Loyalty, usage rate, involvement.
	5.	Benefit – Convenience, value, safety.

Segmentation for B2B
	•	Geographical – Country, region, city.
	•	Firmographic – Industry, firm size, ownership.
	•	Buying Approach – Purchase policy, decision-making.
	•	Behavioral – Loyalty, volume.
	•	Benefits Sought – Price, product quality, service.

Geographic Segmentation
	•	Operate in one or multiple regions.
	•	RIC Swamy BBBO’s Guide to Market Planning:
	1.	Ability to purchase or consume.
	2.	Consumption patterns.
	3.	Exposure to mass media.
	4.	Marketing infrastructure support.

Demographic Segmentation
	•	Variables: Age, gender, income, education, occupation, religion, family size.
	•	Sources: Census, satellite imagery, statistics.
	•	Indian Income Pyramid (2010):
	•	Rich: Above ₹17 lakh.
	•	Middle: ₹3.4–17 lakh.
	•	Aspirers: ₹1.5–3.4 lakh.
	•	Deprived: Below ₹1.5 lakh.

Psychographic Segmentation
	•	Social class.
	•	Lifestyle.
	•	Personality traits.

Behavioral Segmentation
	•	Knowledge.
	•	Attitude.
	•	Usage.
	•	Response to products.
	•	Examples of Ads:
	1.	Diet Coke → Health.
	2.	Garnier → Hair care.
	3.	Nicorette → Anti-smoking.
	4.	Vodafone → Trial pack.
	5.	Maruti → Exchange bonus.

Segmentation Triangulation
	•	Combine psychographic, personality, and behavioral traits.
	•	Segment attractiveness is based on:
	1.	Measurable.
	2.	Accessible.
	3.	Substantial.
	4.	Differentiable.
	5.	Actionable.

Targeting and Positioning
	•	Evaluate segment characteristics (size, growth, profitability).
	•	Analyze competition (strength, resources, intensity).
	•	Consider company objectives and competencies.

Target Market Selection
	•	Target Marketing Strategies:
	1.	Undifferentiated: Mass marketing (e.g., Big Bazaar – local marketing).
	2.	Differentiated: Multiple segments, higher sales but costly (e.g., Amazon, Vodafone).
	3.	Concentrated: Focus on niche (startups, deeper knowledge, e.g., Airtel MyPlan).
