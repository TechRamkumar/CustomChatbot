Marketing Overview

Definition:
Marketing is the process of identifying, anticipating, and satisfying customer needs profitably. It involves understanding customer behavior, creating value, and building strong relationships to drive business growth.

Goal:
- Help business grow by reaching the right customers with the right products or services.
- Maximize customer satisfaction and loyalty while achieving company objectives.

Key Concept:
- You don’t have to sell to everyone.
- Focus on understanding your customer and providing superior value.

Core Marketing Questions:
1. Who are our customers?
2. What do they need?
3. How will we deliver value better than competitors?

---

STP Model (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning)

1. Segmentation:
   - Divide a broad market into smaller groups with common characteristics.
   - Purpose: Tailor products and communication to meet specific needs.
   - Types of Segmentation:
     - Geographic: Region, city, country.
     - Demographic: Age, gender, income, occupation, education.
     - Psychographic: Lifestyle, values, personality.
     - Behavioral: Usage rate, loyalty, purchase patterns.
     - Benefit: Value, quality, convenience, safety.

2. Targeting:
   - Evaluate segment attractiveness (size, growth, profitability, accessibility).
   - Choose segment(s) to serve based on company goals and resources.
   - Strategies:
     - Undifferentiated: Single product for all (mass marketing).
     - Differentiated: Multiple products for different segments.
     - Concentrated/Niche: Focus on a single, profitable segment.

3. Differentiation:
   - Offer unique value that competitors cannot easily replicate.
   - Focus on quality, features, branding, service, or price advantage.

4. Positioning:
   - Create a clear image of your product in the minds of the target customers.
   - Positioning Statements:
     - “For [target audience], [brand/product] is [concept] that [benefit].”

---

Market Segmentation

Purpose:
- Meet segment-specific needs efficiently.
- Optimize resources, reduce costs, and improve customer satisfaction.

Bases of Segmentation:
- Who: Demographics, lifestyle, media habits.
- What: Usage, loyalty, profitability (RFM model: Recency, Frequency, Monetary).
- Why: Needs, preferences, motivations.

Segment Attractiveness (5 A’s):
1. Measurable – Size and purchasing power are quantifiable.
2. Accessible – Can be reached through marketing channels.
3. Substantial – Large enough to be profitable.
4. Differentiable – Distinct from other segments.
5. Actionable – Can effectively serve with marketing strategy.

B2B Segmentation:
- Geographical: Country, region, city.
- Firmographic: Industry, company size, ownership.
- Buying Approach: Decision-making process, policies.
- Behavioral: Loyalty, purchase volume.
- Benefits Sought: Price, quality, service.

---

Targeting & Positioning

Steps to Target:
1. Evaluate segments (size, growth, profitability, competitive strength).
2. Match with company resources and objectives.
3. Choose targeting strategy: undifferentiated, differentiated, or niche.

Positioning Approaches:
- Attribute-based: Focus on key product features.
- Benefit-based: Highlight customer value.
- Use-based: Show product in specific situations.
- Against competitor: Position relative to competitor offerings.

---

Marketing Mix (4Ps & 7Ps)

1. 4Ps of Marketing:

- Product:
  - Tangible or intangible goods/services.
  - Features, quality, branding, packaging, warranty.
- Price:
  - Value exchange between customer and company.
  - Pricing strategies: penetration, skimming, competitive, discounting.
- Place (Distribution):
  - Channels to deliver product: retail, wholesale, online, direct.
  - Logistics, supply chain, inventory.
- Promotion:
  - Communication with target customers.
  - Advertising, social media, PR, sales promotions, events.

2. 7Ps of Marketing (Services Extended):

- People:
  - Employees, customer service, training, interpersonal interactions.
- Process:
  - Procedures and systems delivering the service efficiently.
- Physical Evidence:
  - Tangible proof of service: store environment, brochures, branding.
- Product, Price, Place, Promotion remain same.

---

Other Marketing Concepts

1. Branding:
   - Creating a strong identity and emotional connection with customers.
   - Logos, slogans, brand personality, and reputation management.

2. Customer Relationship Management (CRM):
   - Building long-term relationships using data and personalization.

3. Customer Journey:
   - Awareness → Consideration → Purchase → Retention → Advocacy.

4. Marketing Research:
   - Systematic collection and analysis of market data for informed decisions.
   - Tools: surveys, interviews, focus groups, analytics.

5. Digital Marketing:
   - Online strategies to reach customers: SEO, social media, email, content marketing, PPC ads.

6. Metrics & KPIs:
   - Market share, customer lifetime value, conversion rate, ROI, brand awareness.

---

Summary

- Marketing is customer-centric and strategy-driven.
- STP + Marketing Mix ensures precise targeting and superior value.
- Proper segmentation, differentiation, and positioning create competitive advantage.
- Integrating 4Ps/7Ps with customer insights maximizes satisfaction and profitability.
